# 🔄 BMAD Method - Live Product Assessment Matrix

## Overview

The **ADEO Product Operating Model** and evaluation framework apply throughout the entire product lifecycle, not just pre-development. This matrix shows how to assess **Live products** using the same 7 dimensions and 4 criteria (Valuable, Viable, Usable, Feasible).

**Key Difference**: Live products focus on **optimization, growth, and sustainability** rather than initial development readiness.

## 🎯 Live Product Focus Areas

### Growth & Acquisition
- **User Acquisition**: CAC optimization, channel performance, viral mechanics
- **Activation**: Onboarding optimization, AHA moments, feature adoption
- **Retention**: Churn prevention, engagement loops, loyalty programs

### Customer Success
- **Health Scoring**: Predictive churn indicators, expansion opportunities
- **Support Optimization**: Self-service success, proactive support, knowledge bases
- **Expansion Revenue**: Upsell/cross-sell strategies, account growth planning

### Analytics & Experimentation
- **Product Analytics**: Usage patterns, feature performance, cohort analysis
- **A/B Testing**: Experiment velocity, statistical rigor, learning culture
- **Data-Driven Decisions**: Metrics alignment, hypothesis testing, outcome focus

---

## 📈 Assessment Scoring Guide

### For Live Products:
- **Minimum (60%)**: Product is stable and growing, basic operational excellence
- **Good (75%)**: Strong performance in core areas, competitive positioning
- **Exemplary (90%)**: Industry leader, scalable model, continuous innovation

### Quarterly Review Cadence:
- **Q1 Focus**: Growth metrics and user acquisition
- **Q2 Focus**: Product quality and operational efficiency
- **Q3 Focus**: Financial performance and unit economics
- **Q4 Focus**: Strategic positioning and competitive advantage

## 🚀 Action Framework for Live Products

### Immediate Actions (0-30 days)
- [ ] Conduct baseline assessment across all 7 dimensions
- [ ] Identify top 3 improvement opportunities
- [ ] Set measurable improvement targets

### Short-term (30-90 days)
- [ ] Implement quick wins from assessment
- [ ] Establish regular review cadences
- [ ] Align team incentives with key metrics

### Long-term (90+ days)
- [ ] Build continuous improvement culture
- [ ] Scale successful patterns across portfolio
- [ ] Drive toward exemplary standards
