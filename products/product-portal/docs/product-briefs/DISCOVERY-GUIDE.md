# BMAD Dashboard Discovery Guide

Use this guide during the analysis session with the product management referent. The goal is to gather facts, validate assumptions and capture real user pain points.

## 1. Understanding the Problem Space

1.1. Describe a typical day as a PM for your digital portfolio. What tools do you open first? What tasks dominate your time?

1.2. Where do most stakeholder requests originate? (Slack DM, Slack channel, meeting notes, ServiceNow, quarterly plans, etc.)

1.3. What happens today when a stakeholder asks for something? Who processes the request? How is the work tracked?

1.4. What are the biggest frustrations when handling new requests or prioritizing work?

1.5. Can you describe three recent examples of requests where you felt "I wish I had a better way to…"? What would have helped?

## 2. Tool Usage and Fragmentation

2.1. List all systems/tools you currently use to manage your product (e.g. Jira, Notion, Slack, email, CLI, docs).

2.2. Which of those tools do you consider essential, and which feel like "hacks" or workarounds?

2.3. Do different PMs use different tools? How does that affect collaboration or handoffs?

2.4. When searching for guidance on what to do next (e.g. what workflow to run), what do you do today?

## 3. Workflow Awareness and Expertise

3.1. Are you familiar with BMAD workflows such as "Create PRD" or "Create Vision"? How often do you use them?

3.2. Would you want an interface that lets you browse workflows? What would make that useful vs just running `/bmad-help` in a terminal?

3.3. Do you ever skip workflows because the process feels too heavy or not relevant? Which ones?

## 4. Request Classification

4.1. How do you, or your team, categorize incoming requests today? (bugfix, activation, support, initiative, etc.)

4.2. Who is responsible for categorizing? Are there errors or confusion with types?

4.3. Ideally every request ends up as a Jira ticket—is that the workflow today? What breaks when it doesn’t?

4.4. Would an automatic suggestion help, or is having a dropdown choice sufficient?

## 5. Feature Prioritization and MVP

5.1. If you had to pick one capability that would save you the most time tomorrow, what would it be?

5.2. Which features from the earlier PRD draft do you believe are "must-have" vs "nice-to-have"?

5.3. Is voice input something you would ever use? If not, we can drop it entirely.

5.4. How important is it that non-technical stakeholders use this tool directly?

## 6. Alternatives and Constraints

6.1. What existing tools or processes could we leverage instead of building something new? (Slack bot? Google Form? Notion table?)

6.2. Are there any organizational policies (security, data residency, compliance) we need to consider later?

6.3. What does success look like for you in 3 months? 6 months?

## 7. Open Questions

- Anything we havent covered that might influence the design?
- Who else should we interview as part of discovery?
